The App

This app shows a break-up of vote during US Presidential Elections along the party lines for years 1976 through 2020. You can see the vote break-up for all of US or per state. It allows users to select a year and a state to see further deails.

 

What does it contain?

  • A US states map, that depicts the US Presidential Election Vote break-up by party and state in a user-specified year between 1976 and 2020.
     
  • It also has plots that illustrate how overall party vote proportions have changed with years from 1976 to 2020 in the country as a whole, and in the user-selected state.
     
  • You can also see detailed vote break-up for the state and year you have selected.

Sneak Peak

To give you a little taste, I will show here one of the plots and the code used for it. 

library(plotly); library(htmlwidgets)
data <- read.csv('processedData.csv'); data <- data[data$year == 2020,]
g <- list(scope = 'usa', projection = list(type = 'albers usa'))
plot <- plot_geo(data, locationmode = 'USA-states') %>% 
    add_trace(z = ~percRep, locations = ~state_po, color = ~percRep, colorscale = 'Bluered',text = ~hover, hoverinfo = 'text') %>%
    layout(title=list(text='Year 2020', y=.95, x = 0.02, font=list(size=24, family='Open Sans')), geo = g) %>%
    colorbar(title = 'Percent Vote', y = .78, len=.8, thickness=12, limits = c(0, 100), tickvals = c(10, 90), ticktext = c('90+% \nDem', '90+% \nRep'))
saveWidget(as_widget(plot), 'plot.html')
